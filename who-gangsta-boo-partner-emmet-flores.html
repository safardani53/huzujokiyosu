Who is Gangsta Boo's partner, Emmet Flores?

PROMINENT rapper Gangsta Boo rose to fame as the second female member of the Memphis-based hip-hop group Three 6 Mafia.

Tragically, she passed away on January 1, 2023, and now fans want to know more about her longtime partner, Emmet Flores.

Who is Gangsta Boo's partner, Emmet Flores?

When Gangsta Boo was not in the studio, she could often be found at home, spending time with her partner.

Outside of being known as Gangsta Boo's partner, he is known as the host of the Kush and Chemtrails podcast.

Over the years, the pair would often keep details of their relationship out of the media, but fans could get an inside look after they appeared on WEtv’s Marriage Boot Camp: Hip Hop Edition in 2022.

While on the show, the pair appeared before Judge Lynn Toler in an attempt to work out their issues.

When Judge Toler asked Boo what she wanted from Emmet in the future, she revealed that she wanted more respect.

"Put some respect on my name," she said at the time, "I want him to respect me more."

She then explained: "[I want him] to hear my voice as a Black woman."

"... He always cut me off... He just thinks he knows it all, like when I'm talking he's automatically thinking of a reply."

The judge then asked Emmet why he was there, and he explained that their issues stemmed from miscommunication and control problems.

"When you say certain things to me, you emasculate me," he said.

"I got a lot of insecurities about my manhood growing up without a dad."

Despite the heated conversation, the pair left to show together and appeared to be dating until her sudden death.

What was Gangsta Boo's cause of death?

Gangsta Boo was found dead around 4pm on January 1, 2023, according to Fox13.

Her death was later confirmed by Three 6 Mafia founder DJ Paul on Instagram.

"MAN WE WAS JUS TOGETHER 3 WEEKS AGO," he wrote at the time.

"REST WELL QUENN."

Since her death was announced, several fans and industry colleagues have taken to Twitter to remember the late star.

Among those tributes was one from podcaster and YouTuber Adam John Grandmaison, aka Adam22, who said: "RIP Gangsta Boo. She was one of my first interviews back in 2015 after I ran into her in a bar. Was always a great person, an incredible rapper and an absolute legend. Rest in peace."

Another user added: "Rest Peacefully to the one and only Gangsta Boo #MemphisLegend I’m beyond devastated to hear this news. Thank you for paving the way for female rap from the south."
