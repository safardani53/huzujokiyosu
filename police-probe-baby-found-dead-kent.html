Police probe after baby found dead next to leisure centre in Kent

April 09, 2024
blog

THE body of a baby has been found dead in a park next to a swimming pool in Kent.

Police were called to Broadway, Sheerness just before 1pm today after the tot was discovered. They are now trying to trace the mother as they're concerned for her well being.

Cops cordoned off the area the leisure centre and a nearby church on the seafront.

Officers stood guard as forensic teams in white suits were today gathered evidence from behind police cordons facing near where the River Thames meets the River Medway and joins the sea.

A Kent Police spokesman said: "At 12.48pm on Saturday, 4 March 2017 Kent Police received a report stating that the remains of an infant had been found in Broadway, Sheerness.

"The age and identity is not currently known and neither is the cause of death.

"Officers are at the scene conducting enquiries and are keen to hear from anyone who may have information that can help them establish the circumstances.

"Those that do come forward should know that the information will be handled in a sensitive way."

According to the paper witnesses said five police cars were also there, as well as an ambulance and crews from Kent Fire and Rescue.

Residents said police initially refused to say why the small park, often used as a landing pad for the Kent Air Ambulance, had been closed and was out of bounds.

The unnamed infant's remains were found near St Henry & St Elizabeth Roman Catholic Church, which is close the Marine Parade's junction with Broadway.
