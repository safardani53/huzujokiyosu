Ugly trainers from the 90s are back in fashion and there are some outrageous pairs on the market...

February 15, 2024
blog

WHILE 2017 has produced some bonkers fashion of its own, many of the kooky looks have been inspired by the 90s.

The style overhaul has seen the rise in ugly trainers - which boast chunky soles, clashing colours and a mix of materials.

It's not unusual to spot out-of-the-box designs on the catwalk, but members of the British public seem to be snapping up the footwear.

And the retro kicks don't come cheap - with many retailing for hundreds of pounds.

The trend seems to have been sparked by Balenciaga, who released a £500 range of trainers in September.

When the Spanish luxury brand unveiled the Triple S collection, they were mocked by shoppers for releasing "scruffy lost property" shoes. 

While the "ugly" sneakers didn't appear to have caught on, countless luxury designers have released similar versions.

Prada has jumped on the craze by releasing mesh trainers that are bright blue with a contrasting yellow heel.

Gucci and Dior have also paid subtle homage to the 90s.

While the fashion giants' options are a lot more wearable, both retailers are reportedly planning on rolling out more extreme retro designs this winter.

The high-street has also seen a revival of chunky shoes.

Nike is selling Air Max 95s for around £125 and Reebok's Instapump's retail at approximately £150 depending on design.

As the trend has taken off, budget-friendly shops including Zara have released similar copies.

You can expect to find a pair at the store for around £30.
