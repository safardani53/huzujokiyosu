Early Life & Education

The Mavs shooting guard Reggie Bullock was born on March 16, 1991, to Reggie Bullock Sr. and Danielle Long in Baltimore, Maryland, United States.

Even though he was born in Baltimore, he was raised in Kinston, North Carolina.

Reggie lost his father at six; except for that, not much is known about his parents, but he credits his upbringing and right track to his maternal grandmother Patricia Williams.

He grew up in a drug-infested neighborhood. However, Patricia protected Reggie from the evils in the area.

Bullock had two sisters; Mia Henderson and Keiosha Moore. Unfortunately, on July 16, 2014, his transgender sister Mia was murdered in Baltimore.

His other sister Keiosha was murdered On October 29, 2019, in the same place.

Education

Reggie attended Kinston High School and played basketball for the Kinston High Vikings. He led the team to three state championship games as a high schooler.

He was selected to play in the McDonald’s All-American game in 2010, where he was on the East Team along with Kyrie Irving, Kendall Marshall, Jared Sullinger, and Tobias Harris.

After a remarkable high school basketball, Reggie received scholarships from Wake Forest, Indiana, Ohio State, and Tar Heels, but he committed to the University of North Carolina in January 2008.

Career

After a successful collegiate career, Reggie Bullock declared for NBA Draft and was picked 25th overall in the first round of that year’s draft by the Clippers.

He signed a rookie contract with the team on July 11, 2013, and debuted on October 29, 2019, against the Lakers.

In 2015, Reggie was traded to the Suns in a three-team trade involving the Celtics. The same year in July, he was traded to the Pistons.

Reggie was assigned to the Piston’s D-League affiliate Grand Rapids Drive in December 2015 but was recalled the next day.

He scored a career-high 33 points on 19 December 2018 in an overtime 129-123 win over the Timberwolves while playing for the Pistons.

On February 2019, he was traded to the Lakers and then traded to the Knicks and his current team Dallas Mavericks on August 6, 2021.

Reggie made his Mavs debut in an 87-113 loss to the Hawks on October 21, 2021.
