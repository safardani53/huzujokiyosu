Eric Arthur Blair, better known by his pseudonym George Orwell, is a British novelist, essayist, journalist and critic of Indian descent. His work is characterized by perceptible prose, social criticism, opposition to totalitarianism, and sincere support for democratic socialism. As a writer, Orwell produced literary and poetic reviews, novels and journalism; and is best known for the novel Fables of the Ranch and Incest of Nineteen Eighty-Four. His non-fiction works, including The Road to the Wigan Pier, document his experiences of working-class life in northern England, and A Homage to Catalonia, about his as a Republican soldier in the Spanish Civil War, as well respected as his essays on politics and literature, language and culture. In 2008, The Times ranked George Orwell "50 greatest British writers since 1945".
