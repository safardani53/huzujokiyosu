DIEGO Verdaguer made headlines in January 2022 after it was revealed he had passed away at the age of 70.

He was survived by his wife Amanda Miguel and their three children.

Who is Diego Verdaguer's wife Amanda Miguel?

Born June 1, 1956, Miguel, 65, is known as an Argentine singer.

Over the years, she has released a number of songs including Media Hora, Que Me Das 5 Dias, Amame Una Vez Mas, and El pecado, which peaked at No. 2 on Billboard's Hot Latin Songs chart.

Miguel's interest in music began at a young age and when she was 16, she had moved to Buenos Aires to study at the conservatory.

Shortly after moving, she met Verdaguer and joined his group Mediterríneo before embarking on her own solo career, making her debut with the 1979 single, Papí, Cuando Regreses.

Due to her musical success, she has been able to amass an estimated net worth of $4million, according to Idol Net Worth.

What was Diego Verdaguer's cause of death?

Miguel was the first person to confirm Verdaguer's death in an Instagram post.

"With absolute sadness, we regret to inform the public and friends, that our dear Diego today left his beautiful body, to continue his path and creativity in another form of eternal life," she wrote at the time.

"The whole family is immersed in this pain, so we appreciate your understanding in these difficult times."

Vergaguer reportedly passed away after being diagnosed with Covid the month prior which resulted in hospitalization.

As news started to spread, many took to social media to offer their condolences and pay tribute to the late star.

"My heart is breaking at this devastating news," Sofia Milos wrote. "Rest In Peace my friend Diego."

"RIP Diego Verdaguer. You were a big part of my childhood," a fan added.

"RIP, Diego Verdaguer. What a legend in the Spanish community with his songs," a third wrote.

Who are their children?

Throughout the course of their marriage, the couple raised three children together including Ana, María, and Ximena.

Ana followed directly in her parents' footsteps and became an American singer, songwriter, dancer, and record producer.

Like her mother, her music is very successful in Mexico, and in 2012, Ana was nominated for a Latin Grammy.
