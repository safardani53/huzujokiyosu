Egan Bernal | Early Life 

As Egan Arley Bernal Gómez, the champion cyclist was born to father Germán and mother Flor. Egan was born in Bogotá, Colombia, and brought up in Zipaquirá, a small city in Colombia. 

Interestingly, there are two surnames in the name of Egan. The name is a Spanish name where his father’s or paternal surname is Bernal, and the family name of his mother’s side is Gómez. 

Bernal was not actually from a well-to-do family. Both of his parents worked for the livelihood of the family. His father, German, worked as an employee in a catholic church in Zipaquirá, the Salt Cathedral, whereas his mother worked in a flower factory. 

However, Egan’s father, German, had a keen interest in cycling, and he even was an amateur cyclist. This became the most crucial factor that developed the interest in cycling in Egan. Egan started riding a second-hand bicycle when he was five as his parents couldn’t afford a new one.  

Likewise, at nine, Egan entered a local race and won it. He did this without informing his parents, and the prize for winning this race was a training scholarship. 

Regarding his education, it is known that he studied for a single semester at Universidad de la Sabana in Chia, Colombia.

He then focused on athletics, especially cycling, even in his university days. Likewise, he initially focused on mountain biking which he was good at. He competed in several races across America in his early days and won them.

Racing for professional teams at an early age

Not too later, he caught the eyes of professional cycling teams and bagged a contract with them. He was signed by Gianni Savio to Androni Giocattoli–Sidermec, an Italian pro cycling team.

The year of his signing proved to be quite a good year where he finished inside the Top 20 in the significant cycling contests like La Méditerranéenne, the GP Industria & Artigianato, and Settimana Internazionale Coppi e Bartali and Giro del Trentino.

Similarly, 2017 brought some great results: he finished in the top 10 in Vuelta a San Juan and the Tour de Langkawi.

New Team after Two Years

After competing with the team for two years, Egan signed a contract with Team Sky, a five-year deal. However, at the time of signing, he was still under the contract with Androni Giocattoli–Sidermec, his previous team, so he completed the contract by racing in two more tracks. 

His debut for his new team came in 2018’s edition of Tour Down Under, where he co-led the team. He won the young rider classification on this tour and finished sixth in the overall standings. Likewise, he won the Colombian National Time Trial Championships in the same year. 

Although he had a great start to the first year of his debut with the Team Sky, he suffered a backlash too. Volta, a Catalunya race was happening in Spain where Egan fell on a wet descent in the final stage. The accident caused the fracture of the clavicle and scapula.  

First South American to win Tour De France

In 2019, he competed in the most fantastic cycling race, the Tour De France, as a team co-leader. It was the important race of his career where he had already failed to clinch the title once earlier. However, this time he was determined and also given the role of co-leader. 

On tour full of excitement and thrills, Egan went to win the race being the first-ever Colombian or South American to win the prestigious title. He also won the best young rider competition after winning Tour De France. 

“I’ve won the Tour but I don’t manage to believe it. I need a couple of days to assimilate all this. It’s for my family and I just want to hug them. It’s a feeling of happiness that I don’t know how to describe it.”

Egan Bernal said after the historic Tour De France win. 

Bad Start in 2022

Bernal didn’t have an excellent start to the year 2022. During a training ride in Gachancipá with his teammates, he hit a passenger bus that was stopped. He reportedly struck the bus at a speed of 50 km/hr(31mph).

Later, his team Ineos Grenadiers released a statement saying Bernal had suffered a fractured right femur, a fractured right patella, chest trauma, a punctured lung, and several rib fractures.

It was a dangerous situation as stated by the doctors of Clinica Universidad de La Sabana. The doctors counted a total of 20 fractures in his body and even warned that Bernal may never be able to return to the tracks. They said he might be paralyzed or even die. 

Luckily, he underwent two successful surgeries, and he began to recover. He also shared the progress he is making in his recovery with his fans, friends, and family on his social media.
