American actress China Anne McClain was rumored to be dating late actor Cameron Boyce. She denied the rumors saying she loved him as her good friend.

Denied Dating Late Actor Cameron Boyce

McClain's love life has been under scrutiny with her growing age. She was first linked with her co-star, late actor Cameron Boyce; they had starred together on the show Grown Ups and Descendants 2.

The duo had a firm bond as they knew each other since they were nine years old. Because of this, many suspected that they were dating.

McClain’s Bio: Age, Height

Her Acting Career

The 22-year-old actress started her acting career from the feature film, The Gospel in 2005 that caught Tyler Perry’s attention. And it wasn’t late until he cast her as Jazmine Payne in Tyler Perry’s House of Payne in 2006.

Standing at the height of five feet and three and a half inches (1.61 meters), McClain starred in the romantic comedy movie Daddy’s Little Girls in 2007 with her sisters, Lauryn and Sierra.

She became internationally known when she starred in the Disney Channel television series, A.N.T. Farm. She portrayed a role musical prodigy, Chyna Parks, from 2011 to 2014.

As of now, McClain stars in The CW superhero series Black Lightning as Jennifer Pierce. She has been in the show since 2018. 

Music Career With Sisters

In 2004, McClain and her sisters, Sierra and Lauryn, formed an American R&B girl group, 3mcclaingirls in Atlanta, Georgia. The sisters caught interest in music as they watched their father write and produce songs.

They recorded their first song, Silly Games, which was produced by their father alongside Larry Nix and written by their mother.

The group also recorded the song Daddy’s Girls in 2008 and released it on Myspace.

China and her sisters, Lauryn and Sierra, singing childhood songs on YouTube channel, Lauryn & China (Published on 28 September 2017)

McClain has sung six songs and two solos which debuted in the Billboard 200 at number 29, selling 14000 copies in its first week. The song, Calling All the Monsters, reached 86 on the Billboard Hot 100.
