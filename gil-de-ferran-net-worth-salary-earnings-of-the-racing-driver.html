Racing fans all over the globe are mourning the death of Gil De Ferran and in addition, are interested in his personal life including net worth. The former Indianapolis 500 winner made a name for himself as a racer and team owner.

The world of motor racing is in a state of shock, as one of the prominent names Gil De Ferran has passed away.

Consequently, Gil’s life details like his salary and net worth are among fan curiosity.

Gil De Ferran was born on November 11, 1967, in Paris France. However, he was born to Brazilian parents and hence competed under their flag.

Gil started kart racing at the early age of 13, inspired by his hero Emerson Fittipaldi. He then transitioned to Formula Ford in 1987 and progressed to Formula Three with Edenbridge Racing by 1991.

Moving to Paul Stewart Racing in 1992, de Ferran won the championship with seven victories. This marked the beginning of his successful career, including triumphs in IndyCar racing, such as winning the Indianapolis 500 in 2003.

In terms of his Formula One managerial career, Gil overtook three different stints with BAR-Honda and McLaren with varying degrees of success. Additionally, he also tried his hand in team ownership with De Ferran Motorsports racing in the Le Mans Series.

Gil was loved and admired by the entire racing world for his fearless attitude on track and his fun-loving personality off it.

Gil De Ferran Net Worth: Salary & Earnings

According to most estimates, Gil’s net worth is around the $800,000 to $1.5 million mark. A large chunk of this net worth is accredited to his successful racing career.

As the winner of the 2003 Indy 500 event, he pursed over $1.3 million. Similarly, other successful racing events certainly helped boost his overall net worth and career earnings. 

In terms of his salary, we can predict that they were around the $1 million mark while working as sporting director of McLaren. On top of this Gil’s earnings and net worth are also boosted by the success of his Le Mans team De Ferran Motorsports.

All in all, as one of the most successful racing drivers and managers, Gil has amassed a lot of fame and fortune.

Gil De Ferran’s Death At The Age Of 56

In a recent tragic piece of news, Gil De Ferran passed away at the age of 56 leaving the motorsports world in a state of mourning.

The Brazilian Automobile Confederation (CBA) reported that De Ferran experienced a heart attack at a private racing facility in Opa-Locka, Florida, last Friday. Furthermore, they stated he was promptly taken to a local hospital but did not survive.

Commencing the news, racing legends and personalities have posted tribute messages and appreciation posts on social media. The McLaren F1 team released a statement recognizing Gil’s great career and contributions.

“Everyone at McLaren Racing is shocked and deeply saddened to learn we have lost a beloved member of our McLaren family. Gil was an important and integral part of our Racing team.”

Similarly, other prominent names like Martin Brundle and Roger Penske have also shared their condolences. Brundle remembered Gil’s family and his children. On the other hand, Roger remembered the Brazilian for his career accolades.

Certainly, everyone in the motor racing world loved and admired Gil. The void he has left behind in the sport will be hard to fill. Additionally, well-wishers of the sport will remember his contributions to racing forever.
