A PREDATOR who snatched a seven-year-old girl and bundled her into his car before being caught in the act of a vile sexual assault on his victim has been jailed.

Depraved 42-year-old Gavin Sykes lured the little girl near a park in Huddersfield, West Yorkshire, where she was playing with friends on June 20 last year.

Police received a report about the terrifying abduction at 7.47pm and caught the "sad" sicko 15 minutes later as he fled in his silver Toyota Celia.

The victim was found in the front passenger seat, panicked and crying. had unfortunately already been subjected to a heinous assault by the time she was rescued by officers. "Sad" Sykes was detained at the scene and arrested.

Examiner Live reported that Leeds Crown Court was shown footage from a police body-worn camera in which the victim was panicked and crying when she was rescued from the car.

Sykes, of New Mill Road, Wooldale in Holmfirth, appeared drunk and was arrested at the scene but refused to be breathalysed.

'SAD AND INADEQUATE'

Sykes denied touching her sexually and told officers that the girl had asked him to give her a ride in his sports car and he did not see anything wrong with it.

The victim was reunited with her mother at Holmfirth police station and taken home where she told her horrified mother who was "gripped" in "sheer terror" and police officers that the pervert had slapped her face then touched her 'where he shouldn't'.

'UNUSUAL BRUISES'

She was found to have a small amount of swelling near her left eye, two small, 'unusual' bruises on her chest, a cluster of bruises on her thighs and bruises on her legs and arms.

The court was told she slept in her mother's bed that night and showed symptoms typical of young victims of sexual assault.

Appearing at Leeds Crown Court last month, the paedo finally admitted two counts of sexual assault on a child and one of intent to commit a sexual offence as well as abduction and failing to provide a breath sample.

GRIPPED IN SHEER TERROR

Judge Rodney Jameson QC told Sykes: "You are a sad and inadequate man with limited social skills and little intimacy or sexual contact with others.

"This is shown by your relationship - current or before your remand into custody - with a woman in Thailand which appears to only involve watching her bathe over a video link."

The judge said he was satisfied that Sykes, having failed with the older females, sought out a child victim who would not have had the experience to say no.

He then sentenced Sykes to eight and a half years behind bars.

Sykes was made subject to an indefinite sexual harm prevention order, ordered to sign the sex offender register indefinitely and disqualified from driving for five years.

'EXTREMELY DANGEROUS INDIVIDUAL'

Speaking after the hearing, PC Danni Nugent said: "Sykes is clearly an extremely dangerous individual and we are pleased to see him receive a lengthy custodial sentence for what was a dreadful offence.

"We strongly believe the victim would have come to further harm had on-duty officers not been able to deploy so rapidly and locate his car when they did."

"This incident has clearly been very harrowing for the victim and her family and we hope that seeing this man jailed will be of some measure of comfort for them."
