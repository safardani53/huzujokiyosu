Boy Meets World star Danielle Fishel 'refused to film with female co-stars' on show reboot, Maitland

April 13, 2024

BOY Meets World star Danielle Fishel has been bashed by her former co-star, Maitland Ward, who accused her of ostracizing her female castmates during the show's reboot.

Maitland dished about her relationship with her ex-co-star in her new memoir that dropped on Tuesday, September 6, 2022.

At one point in the book, Rated X: How Porn Liberated Me from Hollywood, the actress turned adult film star slammed Danielle for her supposed treatment of her and the other women on the show.

Maitland, 45, highlighted her interaction with Danielle at a reunion with the entire Boy Meets World cast, where the 41-year-old seemingly ignored her former co-star.

"Danielle didn't speak to me at all, which I thought was odd. She barely acknowledged that I was even there. I hadn't seen her since my wedding, but we hadn't had any ill feelings or bad blood," the ex-TV star wrote.

Maitland theorized that Danielle's behavior might have been mainly directed at her female colleagues.

She claimed that the Disney star wasn't eager to share the screen with the other leading ladies on the Girl Meets World spin-off.

"I don't know if this is true, but there were whispers she didn't want any of the past female cast members to share the screen with her," Maitland said.

"That's why her character would never interact with Trina [McGee]'s when Trina made an appearance on the show, and that's why she didn't like me popping up on the set."

Girl Meets World aired for three seasons on Disney Channel, beginning in 2014.

The show followed Danielle's character, Topanga Lawrence, and her on-screen husband, Corey Mathews, played by Ben Savage, as they navigated adulthood and parenting their two children.

Many of the original Boy Meets World cast made guest appearances, except Maitland, who did not return.

The Bold and the Beautiful alum explained her reasoning in the book, saying that she wanted to move on from her role as Rachel McGuire in the series.

Maitland starred in the popular sitcom for the show's final two seasons, along with Will Friedle, Matthew Lawrence, and Rider Strong.

She has since transitioned to working in the adult entertainment industry, an experience she detailed in her memoir.

Farrah was fired from the Teen Mom franchise after releasing a sex tape in 2013 titled Farrah Superstar: Backdoor Teen Mom.

Maitland harshly critiqued the reality star's work while discussing a sex scene that became a turning point in her porn star career.

"Anyone can leak a sex tape, but the back door was still too taboo to be considered survivable. And MTV reality star Farrah Abraham doesn't count. Backdoor Teen Mom was a disaster," she wrote.
