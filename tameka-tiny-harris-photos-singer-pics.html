Tameka, ‘Tiny’ Harris is an American singer, songwriter and actress from Georgia. She rose to fame in the 1990s as one of four members of the multi-platinum R&B girl group, Xscape. Xscape was discovered by producer Jermaine Dupri, who signed the group to his then new record label, So So Def Recordings. Tiny’s also known for her solo music contributions. In 2000, she won a Grammy Award for her writing contribution on TLC’s hit, ‘No Scrubs’. Tiny and Xscape bandmate Kandi Burruss, also have writing credits on Ed Sheeran’s hit song, ‘Shape of You’. Xscape had its first performance together in over 18 years at the BET Awards on June 25, 2017. In September 2017, the group announced a reunion tour with Monica and Tamar Braxton as openers. On March 2, 2018 ‘XSCAP3’ released their EP titled “Here for It”. In April 2019, Tiny told HollywoodLife that she plans to release new solo music inspired by her relationship with husband, T.I. The two married in July 2010 in Miami, Florida. They have three children together — sons Clifford ‘King’ Joseph Harris III and Major Philant Harris and their youngest, a daughter, Heiress Diana Harris. Tip has two sons, Messiah Ya’Majesty Harris and Domani Uriah Harris, along with a daughter, Deyjah Imani Harris from previous relationships. Tiny has a daughter, Zonnique Jailee Pullins from a previous relationship. In December 2016, Tiny filed for divorce from T.I., but the couple never went through with it. She told HollywoodLife in April 2019, that Tip is the love of her life and that they were living under the same roof again.
